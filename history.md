  <h1>From Ancient Traditions to Modern Celebration</h1>
  <p class="lead">Chinese New Year has evolved over thousands of years. While celebrations look different by region, the values of family, renewal, and good wishes remain central.</p>

<section class="section">
  <h2>Timeline</h2>
  <div class="timeline">
    <article class="timeline-item">
      <p class="kicker">Ancient agricultural origins</p>
      <p>Early communities celebrated the end of winter and prepared for spring planting.</p>
    </article>
    <article class="timeline-item">
      <p class="kicker">Han dynasty traditions</p>
      <p>Many ceremonial practices became more formal and widespread.</p>
    </article>
    <article class="timeline-item">
      <p class="kicker">Tang and Song cultural growth</p>
      <p>Lantern displays, markets, and performances became major public events.</p>
    </article>
    <article class="timeline-item">
      <p class="kicker">Global celebration era</p>
      <p>Today the festival is celebrated worldwide with parades, school programs, and family gatherings.</p>
    </article>
  </div>
</section>

<section class="section">
  <h2>Legend of Nian</h2>
  <p>
    One famous legend tells of a monster called Nian that feared loud sounds, bright lights, and the color red. This story helps explain why fireworks, lanterns, and red decorations became iconic New Year symbols.
  </p>
</section>
